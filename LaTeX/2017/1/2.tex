Let $f\colon\mathbb{R}\to(0,\infty)$ be a differentiable function, and suppose that there exists a constant $L>0$ such that 
$$
\bigl|f'(x)-f'(y)\bigr| \leq L\bigl|x-y\bigr|
$$
for all $x,y$.
Prove that 
$$ \big(f'(x)\big)^2<2Lf(x) $$
holds for all $x$.
